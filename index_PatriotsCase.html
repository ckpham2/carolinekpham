Patriots & Paws
Website Redesign

Case Study - October 2020

Providing Veterans/ Active Military / Reservists and their families in SoCal basic home furnishing for newly acquired residences

Timeline

2 weeks

Tools

Miro, Figma, Photoshop & Illustrator

Role

User Research, User Experience, Interface & Visual Design

Team

Ashley Mosqueda & Sulki Kim

The Problem

We have observed that the current Patriots & Paws website isn't gaining enough foot traffic, which leads to a decrease in volunteer support for the organization. How might we improve Patriots and Paws' website so that they see an increase in volunteers and donations for their future programs?

The Solution

Redesign Patriots & Paws’ website in order to better convey the organization’s mission in a simple, visually appealing manner where users are able to easily understand, donate, and view the website through their desktops and mobile devices.

Who is Patriots & Paws?

Patriots & Paws is a nonprofit based in Southern California that provides Veterans/Active Military/Reservists and their families throughout Southern California basic home furnishings for newly acquired residences. Additionally, at Patriots and Paws for those Veteran/Military service folks that are interested, they can be matched up with a rescued companionship animal.

User Research

We interviewed 6 individuals and gathered data from 15 survey participants. Through conducting our research we hoped to gain insight into what attracted users to pick certain nonprofits over others, their volunteering/ donating experience and what they believe makes a nonprofit website easy to navigate through as well as appealing.

What factors do you consider while researching a non-profit organization to support?

“I consider what services they provide, what population demographic they serve when researching non-profits.”

What would motivate or interest you in donating or volunteering at a non-profit?

“I’m motivated and interested in supporting nonprofits that have a personal tie to or because I believe in their cause.”

Our research showed that most were between the ages 27 and up, equally male & female and many were interested in helping otheres or supporting military personnel and their families. After conducting our interviews and survey, we synthesized our data into affinity diagrams in order to pin point our users’ needs, goals and frustrations, We found that many users felt frustrated if the information was outdated, many pages or links are dead end. Users also provided insights to what they wished to see on a nonprofit website as well as being able to easily navigate and find how they can donate, volunteer, background on the non-profit and who the nonprofit helps. We also conducted a competitor analysis against a few of our direct and indirect competitors to gain further insight on the users’ through process.

From our data, we were able to create our user persona and concluded that users navigating through Patriots & Paws were interested in making a difference through volunteering or donating as well as raising awareness in supporting veterans and military personnel. Pain points that would cause them to stop visiting the website would be overwhelmed by the amount of information presented on the website and not being able to get accurate, up-to-date information.

Definition & Ideation

To better understand our user’s journey and needs, we created a storyboard to help guide us on our redesign ideation and brainstorming.

We then evaluated the current website of Patriots and Paws using the Heuristic evaluation method to identify UI patterns and what need to be redesigned in order better convey Patriots & Paws mission in a simple and appealing manner. Here’s what we found:

1. Busy Imagery
2. Too much text causing information to get lost
3. Inconsistent typography
4. Redundant information

Then we used card sorting to reorganize and rename the sections of the navigation bar, Afterwards we mapped out our Site Map to begin the redesign of Patriots & Paws’ website.

Testing & Wireframing

Once we had our user flow, we began creating our lo-fi wireframes and developed a user testing plan for our prototype. We had 6 users test our intital prototype. We asked our users to complete 3 tasks.

Our team’s goal was to determine whether Patriots & Paws website functions as intended while being easy for users to navigate through. We asked our users to complete 3 tasks, as illustrated above.

All of our users were able to successfully work through each task. However, they had some suggestions that we could improve upon on our website design & navigation.

1. Call to visibility to important buttons for better noticeability.
2. Rename certain tabs for clearer navigation. (Ex: Ways to Give)
3. Relocate some of the buttons. (Ex: “Donate Now” towards the top of the page more for easier finding).

Overall, users liked how easy it was for them to navigate along with the information being clear and straightforward on each page. They found the donate button on every page to be helpful in making it quick and easy to donate without having to go back to the Donation page.

We also conducted A/B testing for our Volunteer Sign Up Page. Our testing results showed that those shown the pop up selection found it to be easier to sign up and select their volunteer option as seen to the left in the image above rather than seening the calendar and volunteer sign up options on the same page (see example on the right in image above).

Once we completed our user testing on the lo-fidelity prototype, we designed a Style Guide for our High Fidelity Prototype. In creating our prototype we made sure to use and follow a 4pt grid with 20px gutter/20px margin for our mobile version & a 12pt grid with 20px gutter/93px marging for our web version.

After all of our user testing and designing our style guide, we applied the styles to our design to produce our final prototype of our mobile and web version of Patriots & Paws new website. The UI design was focused to support and convey Patriots & Paws message and important information in a easy, visually appealing manner. We wanted it to have a color scheme that still alluded to the American Flag colors without being overpowering and unbearable.

Key Takeaways & Future Opportunities

Through our design process, we learn the importance of how words and they way things are worded on certain pages contribute to the user’s experience of being able to easily navigate and find information they are looking for. In order for us to provide the best user experience and keep users’ attention, iterating was key to ensure a seemless design and navigation experience as well through A/B testing to learn the best design users were receptive to.

Our plan for the future is to futher improve our design and the user’s experience by continuing to develop the web version by further develop the navigation bar, create other pages (FAQ, Contact Us, etc.), while also adding testimonials by gathering quotes, photos, etc. from families & military personnel that have successfully been helped through Patriots & Paws. We plan to also gather testimonials from staff and volunteer that have contributed to helping veterans, active duty military personnel and reservists at Patriots & Paws.
